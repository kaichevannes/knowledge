We don't want to prevent the risk factors, we want to address the risk.
Have a conversation with your partner about your concerns. Making sure your
relationship stays healthy: listen to your partner, physically connect with
your partner.

## Risk factors
1. **Sexual Narcissism**: when we view sex as a form of ego, self-esteem,
  conquest.
2. **Emotional Vulnerability**: externally regulating our emotions e.g. alcohol
  use. Sex can be used as an emotional regulator.
3. **Traditional Gender Roles**: in breadmaker and homemaker relationships, both
  partners are at a greater risk for infidelity. It's easier to cheat on your
  partner when you are separated in your lives.
4. **Work Situations**: higher rates of travel and access to co-workers.

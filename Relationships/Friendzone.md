"I'm like right here... why don't you date me?!"

We get friendzoned when we are scared of rejection and invest, invest, invest
into the relationship to stack the deck in our favour, to get the person to
like us: picking up from airport, getting gifts, helping with a clogged toilet.
The problem is that these things are not explictly romantic, the context isn't
right. Getting someone to like you is very different from evoking attraction.

As we invest in a relationship more and more, we can become resentful: feel we
are owed something, which can become toxic.

## What to do
Let the person know your feelings: "Hey I know we've been friends for a while,
but I'm starting to have feelings that are kind of unexpected, some romantic
feelings about you. I'm thinking about you in a different way -- are you
interested in exploring where this could go?". Pose it to your friend as a
problem for the friendship to solve. If they say no: "I'm glad that you told
me and I'm glad that you feel that way -- I don't feel the same way, I'm not
interested in exploring something with you." The only thing you can do is to
move on, otherwise there is no space for anyone else.

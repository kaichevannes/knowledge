The people that you are attracted to, and more generally the things that you are
attracted to, are not necessarily good for you. The classic relationship is
between the anxiously attached person and the avoidantly attached person. One
person pulls away and the other chases. If you are avoidant, the best way to not
be abandoned is to have somebody constantly chase after you.

## Resolving Attachment Issues
Pay attention to what you are attracted to in this person, and how it relates to
something within you. Conversely, if you dislike someone, try to identify those
things that you dislike the most in them and find them within yourself.

As we start to heal internally, unhealthy (compulsive) attractions will start
to melt away.

## Anxious Attachment (25%)
"You're going to leave, you're going to leave, you're going to leave. I know
you're going to leave me". Eventually they do. Can use sex to draw the other
person in.

## Avoidant Attachment (20%)
Self-reliant and independent -- "It all comes down to me and I cannot rely
on anybody else". Emotional connection is "a dependency that I don't want".
Maintain emotional distance by being cold and withdrawing as a relationship
becomes closer, running away from emotional intimacy; citing reasons like having
to work late, having to travel. Sex is used as a way to keep emotional distance,
it becomes transactional.

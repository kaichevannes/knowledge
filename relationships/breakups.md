# Breakups

The intensity of negative emotion is inversely correlated with developing PTSD.
This means that the more it hurts, the more you heal. If you run away from your
feelings then you are more likely to be traumatized by the breakup. 

## Closure
It's  important to get closure, and we can get this closure without the other
person. We have rituals like funerals and eulegies when someone dies, and we
also have a set of rituals for moving past relationships: moving things out,
burning pictures, having a closure conversation with the person.

## Grieving the relationship
The relationship shapes your perception of yourself, of life, and of the future.
Instead of getting attached to that future, we should reflect on: "Who was I
when the relationship started?", "Who am I now that the relationship has
ended?", "How have I changed over the course of the relationship?", "What is the
future that I saw with this person?", "Now that this future I saw is gone, what
does this mean about my life, and my future?"

Trauma leaves us frozen in time. Moving on from breakups is literally moving on,
looking to a new future and thinking about what comes next.

## Communicating with your ex
As long as we persist the communication patterns and habits from the
relationship, it is hard to get the person out of your life, and more
importantly: hard to create space for the next person. It's important to limit
your contact.

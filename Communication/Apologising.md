## How to ruin an apology
1. I'm sorry, but...
Regardless how you end the sentence this means "I'm sorry, but I don't owe you
an apology".

2. I'm sorry you feel that way.
"I have nothing to apologise for, you feeling bad is your problem and I've done
nothing wrong".

3. The mystifying apology
Trying to apologise by doing things like leaving them alone, basically fooling
yourself that you've apologised when you haven't actually addressed it.

4. Asking for forgiveness
An apology has nothing to do with them accepting it, a good apology is saying
something like "This is the specific thing I've done wrong that has hurt you, I
understand why it was wrong now, and I will change my behaviour in the future.
I'm sorry". If you say "Please forgive me for this thing I did" they are no
longer in control of forgiving you, you are demanding it from them.

5. The intrusive apology
When you have wronged someone so much, and they clearly aren't ready to talk
to you, and you still try to apologise to them. Sometimes an apology before
you change isn't enough, and you should never apologise to try to make yourself
feel better.

## The positive ingredients of an apology
1. Never saying "but" in the apology, at all, ever
2. Focus on your own actions, never the other person. Instead of "im sorry you
   were hurt by what I said" its "I'm sorry for what I said. It was insensitive
   and hurtful"
3. Don't bring up your own pain or remorse, it is about the other person, not
   your own remorse. Probably a bit is fine but it shouldn't overshadow how the
   other person feels
4. It doesn't matter who is to blame
5. Backup your apology by actually changing. If you are eloquently apologising
   over and over for the same thing, that is clearly going to piss them off.
6. Don't use the apology as an excuse "I've already apologised for this, please
   just drop it" probably means you don't actually understand what the problem
   is

Focus on the other person. Listen to them without being defensive. Earnestly
try to understand what it was that has hurt the other person and change
yourself so that it doesn't happen again.

# Empathy and Validation

Empathy and validation are ways that we show we are listening to the other
person in a conversation.

## Empathy

Empathy is resonating with someone's feelings "That sounds nerve wracking."
Empathetic listening allows us to present to the other person that we are giving
them our full attention, that we understand what they are saying and what it
means to them.

## Validation

Validation is letting someone know that their reaction is appropriate "Getting
the metro in San Francisco while tired does sound genuinely challenging." You
can validate someone using an example: "I felt the same way navigating the
London underground last weekend." It's important that we don't minimise the
validation by saying something like "but it's not that hard". Instead we could
say "Strangely though, it was much more manageable than I expected. Hopefully
the metro is similar in that way."
